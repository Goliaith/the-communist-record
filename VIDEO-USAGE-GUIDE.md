# Video Usage Guide — The Communist Record

This separate guide explains how to use the website (https:// or local version) as source material when creating videos about communism.

**Important**: The public website itself does **not** contain script templates, production tools, or video-specific sections. It is a clean historical reference.

## Recommended Approach

1. Start with the Home page for the big picture and key statistics.
2. Use Origins + Timeline for the narrative backbone.
3. Use Regimes and Human Cost for concrete examples and numbers.
4. Use Why It Fails for the analytical "why" section.
5. Use Modern Risks for the relevance to today (the "so what" for a Western audience).
6. Always pull citations from the Sources page.

## Suggested High-Level Video Structure (example)

- Hook with the scale of the human cost (100M+)
- Brief ideology (Origins)
- How it played out in practice (Timeline + Regimes)
- The results (Human Cost)
- Why this outcome was predictable (Why It Fails)
- Why it still matters (Modern Risks)
- Conclusion with sources and call to study primary material

## Key Stats to Consider Using
- ~94–100 million deaths (Black Book of Communism and related research)
- Breakdowns: China (~65M), USSR (~20M), Cambodia (~2M), etc.
- Specific events: Holodomor, Great Leap Forward, Cultural Revolution, Khmer Rouge

## Best Practices
- Quote sources on screen or in description.
- Use the site's scannable structure to pull short, powerful facts.
- Distinguish between stated ideals and actual historical results.
- For the modern section, focus on documented ideological influences rather than unsubstantiated conspiracy claims.
- Always include the site's disclaimer language or your own clear sourcing.

## How to Cite
Link to the relevant page or the Sources page. Example:
"According to *The Black Book of Communism* and other historians, communist regimes caused approximately 100 million deaths in the 20th century. See the full record at [The Communist Record]."

## After Using the Site
Consider reading the primary books listed in Sources for deeper material:
- The Black Book of Communism
- Works by Conquest, Dikötter, Applebaum, Solzhenitsyn
- Original texts (Communist Manifesto, etc.)

This site is intended as a starting point and reference, not the final word.

Good luck with your video.
